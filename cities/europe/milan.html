Milan

At A Glance

Milan is mostly set up as a circle with the center of the city showcasing the Duomo and having every shopping brands you can imagine. In the north, the areas of Brera and Isola are more artsy and modern areas having many rooftop lounges and great cafes. At night don’t forget to take a trip down to Navigli. This canal area is sprawling with bars and people at all hours of the day. If you are a foodie this also one of the best cities you can visit, having fantastic pizza, pasta, and other Milanese food at almost every corner. Dinners also happen quite late in Milan, and all of Italy, with an aperitivo as a bite before dinner. Aperitivo is a 9 euro all you can eat buffet that is meant to be a snack but can be your dinner if you’re so inclined. Known for being a fashion capital, Milan is that and so much more. Check out what all you can do in this amazing city below!

      Clubs

      1. Gattapardo

        Venue 5/5 | Price: 4/5 | Student night: Tuesdays
        Located in an old church and having a gigantic chandelier at the center of the club, Gattopardo is sure to wow anyone that enters. With great music, this is sure to be a hit anytime you go out. There is a semi-strict dress code and it can be difficult to get in so plan accordingly.

      2. Prive / Armani Club

        Venue 4/5 | Price: 5/5
        Located at the Armani Hotel, Prive is definitely the fanciest clubbing experience in Milan. With big name DJs occasionally playing here it is a great time, but one of the priciest clubs in Milan. Strict dress code and long lines so plan accordingly.

      3. Volt

        Venue 3/5 | Price: 3/5 | Student night: Thursdays
        If hip-hop music is your vibe, this is the club for you as the music is exclusively hip-hop/rap. The venue is a bit small but big enough to have a good time.

      4. TOM

        Venue 3/5 | Price: 3/5 | Student nights: Mondays / Thursdays
        The most local clubbing experience you can get, TOM will be sure to be packed with Locals and be playing Italian music to the late hours of the night. A little difficult to get into to so plan accordingly.

      5. Le Banque

        Venue 4/5 | Price: 3/5 | Student night: Saturdays
        A variety of music, decently priced, and located very close to the city center, Le Banque is an easy choice if you’re looking for a place to go out in Milan.

      6. Just Cavalli

        Venue 4/5 | Price: 5/5
        This outdoor venue is famous for its stylish club but is a good place for a fancy aperitivo as well.

      7. Old Fashion

        Venue 3/5 | Price: 4/5 | Student night: Wednesdays
        A good student club with relatively well priced drinks. The small venue opens up into an indoor/outdoor setup in the summer months.

      Bars

      Check out some of these bars below, but if you want to see where the night takes you check out Navigli Grande where you can find fun bars across the canal streets.

      1. Nottingham Forest

        Price: 5/5 | Venue: 5/5| Student Night: Tuesday
        Regularly named in any list that compiles the 50 best bars in the world this Bar is a must visit in Milan. The drinks are amazing and the containers they come in are just as exciting. Note that you can’t make reservations and it as always busy so come here early or be prepared to wait.

      2. Terrazza Aperol / Dummo 21

        Price: 5/5 | Venue: 5/5| Student Night: Tuesday
        Both of these terrace bars give you an amazing view of Milan’s famous Duomo. Both bars are located next to each other and you can tell from the Duomo square which one to go to that day. The drinks and music is good but can be pricey with all drinks costing 15 Euros.

      3. The Botanical Club

        This place is a cocktail bar, restaurant, and they even have time to be a gin distillery. The drinks here are very good. Good aperitivo spot.

      4. Luca & Andrea

        Small bar in Navigli where the bartenders Luca & Andrea make a drink special for you, no menu.

      5. La Fontanella

        If you ever want to see what a College bar would look like in Milan look to further than here. This rowdy student bar is a fun place almost any day of the week. With the main beer menu and whacky containers – think a train or 10-liter science beaker – this is a great place for big groups.

      6. Bamboo Bar @ Armani Hotel

        An upscale bar located at the Armani Hotel in Milan. Great view, nice place, but can be a bit pricey.

      7. The Doping Club @ The Yard

        This place is cozy with a décor. The drinks here are very good, and the bartenders take great pride in making the drinks. This place is definitely worth a visit.

      8. Backdoor 43

        Contender for the world’s smallest bar, there is only space for 4 people inside the bar. The bartender makes drinks based on what you like instead of using a menu. If you want to enter go to their website and book the bar out for you and your group.

      9. Radio Rooftop Bar

        Cool rooftop bar offering great views of Milan

      10. Ceresio 7

        Cool rooftop bar offering great views of Milan

      11. Mag

        Cool bar in Navigli with a good drink menu

      Italian Food (Traditional)

      1. Cacio e Pepe

        The famous Roman-style pasta made to perfection here. Can get busy so make a reservation

      2. Osteria della Lanterna

        This restaurant has no menu, just whatever great food the chief (who is a very nice lady) is making that day. This is a small restaurant with only one round of tables so making a reservation is a must.

      3. Paper Moon

        An semi-upscale place with various Italian dishes. Good if you want a mix of different foods.

      4. Da Oscar

        Known for its seafood dishes, but everything here is great – especially anything with Da Oscar sauce. The plates here are very big so be hungry when you go.

      5. Al Cantinone

        A local favorite, the food, and service here are both out of this world.

      6. Drogheria Milanesi

        A “fancy” restaurant with a nice atmosphere, good food, and good drink selection.

      7. Cracco (In Galleria Vittorio Emanuele)

        This place is a bit overpriced, and touristy, due to it being in the Emanuele Galleria, but that doesn’t take away from the fact that the food is really good.

      8. Miscusi

        A fast-casual pasta spot, Miscusi is a choose your own pasta spot with nothing but good options. Decently priced as well this is a great place for lunch or dinner any night of the week.

      Italian Food (Pizza)

      Italian Pizza is typically very thin with a soft base and mix of different sauces and cheeses. The only meets you will see on pizza here is salami or prosciutto.

      1. Gino Sorbillo

        Some of the best pizza you will ever have. Sorbillo has more pizza options than other places on this list.

      2. Pizza Am

        Only having 3-4 different pizza choices you can’t really go wrong with any choice. There is usually a line here but do not worry while you wait you are given free welcome pizza samples and prosecco!

      3. Piz

        Owned by the same people as Pizza Am there is much of the same here. 3 pizza options – Classic, Red Sauce only, and White cheese only – and great samples as you wait for your table to open up.

      4. Pizzium

        Great pizza with a good selection.

      5. Berbere

        A nice casual pizza spot located in Isola and Navigli

      6. Spontini

        Another Milan staple, Spontini is known for its single thick Sicilian slice pizza. Very popular this is a good place to sit down or grab a quick bite to go.

      Touristy but Worth it

      1. Duomo / Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II

        The famous Duomo cathedral and shopping center. These two places are located right next to each other in the center of the city. The Duomo is a must-visit, and it possible to go inside and on top of the famed building, if you are willing to wait on sometimes incredibly long lines.

      2. The Golden Rectangle of Fashion

        In one of the fashion capitals of the world, this area of the city has every designer store you can imagine.

      3. The Last Supper

        The famed Leonardo DaVinci painting. Make sure to book this in advance as is almost always sold out.

      4. Explore Brera

        This picturesque area of town has many local shops and great eateries everywhere you turn.

      5. See a football match at San Siro Stadium

        Home of AC Milan football team a game here never has a dull moment. Tickets can range in price depending on the opposing team, but you can always find a good deal.

      6. Go to Starbucks Reserve

        Yes, the Milan Starbucks, Starbucks Reserve, is something to check out. It is the biggest Starbucks in the world with some of the most exotic coffee beans ever. Coffee aficionados should definitely pay a visit to this one-of-a-kind Starbucks

      7. Castello Sforzesco & Parco Sempione

        The former castle turned into a museum and park is a great place to visit for a relaxing day in breezy Milan weather.

      8. Visit 10 Corso Como Cafe

        The large complex with so much beauty to explore. There is an art gallery, bookstore, shopping, and café. Located in northern Milan you can easily spend an entire day here.

      9. Explore Navigli

        Navigli is Italian for Canal, which is where the name for this area full of shops, restaurants, and bars comes from. This area will be bustling all hours of the day and night.

      10. Visit La Scala

        The famed opera house, this place is a great way to experience Italian opera with a world-class show guaranteed.

      Day Trips

      1. Lake Como

        Distance from Milan: 40 minute train | Minimum Time needed: 1 day
        The famed resort area is known for its scenery and beautiful view of the alps it is as pretty as the pictures show. While here you can also visit the town of Bellagio where the huge hotels get their name from.

      2. Cinque Terre

        Distance from Milan: 3 hour train | Time needed: 2 days
        Made up of 5 towns, translated to Cinque Terre, this is a picturesque and beautiful place to unwind enjoy the small towns and Italian culture. You should see all 5 towns if you get the chance, with a train that goes through them or a 6 hour hike. If you only have time for a few Vernazza and Manarola are the prettiest.

      3. Verona

        Distance from Milan: 1.25 hour train | Time needed: 1 day
        The home of Romeo and Juliet. Here you can see the balcony known as “Juliet’s house” and other tourist spots. There is also plenty of shopping to do here.

      4. Bologna

        Distance from Milan: 1.5 hour train | Time needed: 1 day
        Bologna is a lively, historic area in northern Italy. Its main square is lined with arched colonnades, cafes and medieval and Renaissance structures such as City Hall, the Fountain of Neptune and the Basilica di San Petronio.

      5. Genova

        Distance from Milan: 1.5 hours train | Time needed: 1/2 day
        The place where Pesto was created and where yachts come and dock their boats Genova is a quaint but lively city. On the way to Cinque Terre, it is a good place to stop and break up the trip.

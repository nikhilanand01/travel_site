Berlin

At A Glance

Berlin is a large city with different districts making up the city. At the center of the city is Mitte with many cafes, restaurants, museums, and clubs here. You can think of Mitte as the Soho of Berlin. Another hip area with lots of arts and clubs is Kreuzberg, located in East Central Berlin. This city has been at the center at some of the most important historical moments in modern human history, which you can feel when walking around. Now it is a modern metropolis with a very interesting art scene. The East Side Gallery is very popular but you can beautiful street art all across the city. Berlin also has one of the craziest nightlife scenes in all of Europe. The strict entry rules can make it seem intimidating at first, but go with the flow, and you’re bound to have a great time. Check out what all you can do in this fantastic city below!
*Most of the prominent tourist sights can be seen on the free walking tours. Highly recommend doing that*

      Clubs

      Berlin clubs are mostly techno clubs, but sometimes you can find a variety of music if you look for a specific vibe. It can also be difficult to get into certain clubs but below are some tips to help you get in.

      1. Berghain

        Difficulty of getting in 11/10
        The best and most exclusive club in Berlin. Known for their infamous bouncer and door policy, people have waited in line for up to 3 hours only to be turned away by the famous bouncer. A small club with intense techno music.
        *Go with a maximum of 3 other people and follow the tips above.

      2. Tresor

        Difficulty: 8/10
        This 3-level club was once a power plant. The bottom floor is the heavy techno music and the 2nd floor has lighter electronic music.

      3. Club Soda

        Difficulty: 6/10
        This is a huge club is one of the few non-techno clubs in Berlin. There are often nights where ladies are free and get free drinks. There is also free drink tickets on Fridays and Saturdays before 11pm.

      4. Kater Blau

        Famous Berlin club, with great DJs and lights.

      5. Kit Kat Club [XXX]

        A famous sex club. Every Saturday night and on special occasions they have a fetish or themed parties, other times it’s more “normal.” Here people wear crazy outfits (or nothing at all), leather, and sometimes have sex in the open. It’s a notorious Berlin experience.

      6. Suicide Circus

        A fun techno club but can sometimes be full of tourists.

      7. Matrix

        This place has a more international vibe compared to the “Berlin experience” which is why you will see a lot of tourists there. There will be hip-hop music in the mix here.

      Bars

      1. Das Hotel

        Although this translates to “The Hotel” it is not in or located near any hotel. Rather it is a lowkey bar with good vibes, and better drinks.

      2. Monkey Bar

        Amazing view of Berlin in this indoor/outdoor rooftop bar. Great drinks but lines can be long and service can be rude.

      3. Klunkerkranich

        This rooftop bar has very cool music and above the bar is a new area with a dance floor that is more for people who want to dance and not socialize as much. It can be hard to find, but if you go through the side door of the mall, go straight up the elevator, it should be right there.

      4. Limonadier

        Their slogan is ‘we love good drinks’ and it shows. This cocktail bar has amazing drinks with a lot of variety. The bar staff is in dapper attire and the cocktails attractively presented as well.

      5. Sharlie Cheen

        Great music, cool layout, and hipster vibe.

      6. Madame Claude

        In this bar, everything is upside down. It is a very cool bar with bands playing live often.

      7. Luzia

        A fun bar that is a good place to start your night out.

      Museums

      1. Museum Island

        In the Mitte District, this is an area that contains 5 museums. The museums are The New Musem, The Old Museum, Old National Gallery, The Bode Museum, and Pergamon Museum. There is also a nice park to hang out at the center of this area.

      2. Holocaust Memorial Museum

        The Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe, also known as the Holocaust Memorial, consists of concrete slabs on a sloping field above ground. The bottom is an attached underground "Place of Information" shows the names of approximately 3 million Jewish Holocaust victims.

      3. Urban Nation

        Berlin street art museum. Showcases great work and the importance of street art in Berlin.

      4. Topography of Terror

        This museum goes into depth about the horrific actions of the Nazis during the WWII era. The building also has historical significance with it being was the SS Reich Main Security Office, the headquarters of the Sicherheitspolizei, and Gestapo to name a few.

      Touristy but Worth it

      If you take the free walking tour you will get to see all these places and more with great insight into what makes this city so special.

      1. Brandenburg Gate

        One of the best-known landmarks in Germany, this “gate” was a major milestone to cross for many historical figures and the gate that soldiers would pass through when leaving for and returning from battle.

      2. East Side Gallery

        This is a piece of the Berlin Wall that has been turned into an art gallery with some truly amazing street art on it.

      3. Checkpoint Charlie

        The 3rd checkpoint where military members could pass between the American West and Soviet East sectors of Berlin.

      4. The Jewish Holocaust Memorial

        The Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe, also known as the Holocaust Memorial, consists of concrete slabs on a sloping field above ground. The grid structure allows you to walk around and feel the energy at the memorial.

      5. Reichstag

        The building where Berlin’s parliament meets. There is a clear dome on the top of the building which you can enter and walk around to get a great view of the city and the building. It is free to enter but tickets sell out fast so do this in advance.

      6. Mauerpark

        On Sundays there is a large flea market where you can find a variety of nicknacks and gifts. In the warmer months there’s live performances and even sometimes karaoke!

      7. Olympiastadion

        Now the home of the Hertha soccer team, this was previously the location of the infamous 1936 Berlin Olympics where Jesse Owens won gold in front of the Nazi regiem. There's a lot of history here and if you go to watch a game you’ll definitely feel it.

      8. View Berlin from the top of the TV Tower

        The Berliner Fernsehturm is the TV tower and it is the highest structure in all of Germany. You can go to the top to get a view of the city, grab a drink at the bar, or eat at the revolving restaurant.

      Food

      1. Dӧner

        This has become a staple in Berlin and for good reason. You can get it almost anywhere at any time, for pretty cheap too. If you pass by a shop be sure to grab a döner with you.

      2. Markthalle Neun - Kreuzberg

        An indoor food hall with great food and beer options. Be careful as prices for food can add up, quick. Closed on sundays.

      3. NENI Berlin

        This is the restaurant across from Monkey bar that serves Israeli and Mediterranean food. You can get a good view of the city from here as well.

      4. Schnitzel

        A schnitzel is meat, usually thinned by pounding with a meat tenderizer, that is fried. It’s a german classic and is a must try if you’re visiting.

      5. Silo Coffee

        A good brunch place with great food that is highly likely to end up on your Instagram feed. On Fridays there is a neighboring flea market where you can go if you have to wait for a table.

      6. Sticks'n'Sushi

        A nice sushi place with great food

      7. Burgermeister

        A burger joint with a good variety on the menu.

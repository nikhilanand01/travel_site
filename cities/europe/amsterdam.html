Amsterdam

At A Glance

The city of Amsterdam is set up around its inner, middle, and outer canals. The city center is all inside the inner canal ring. As soon as you step outside the Central station you will be in the heart of the city with the Dam Square, Shopping, Coffeeshops, and the Red Light District all right there. Moving farther out the center, Leidseplein and Rembrandtplein are where you want to be if you’re looking for a fun night out. The south houses the city’s museum district, Museumplein, and the Albert Cuyp Market, where you can get some of the best Stroopwaffels and Poffertjes in the world. Biking is the most common transportation method, but with fantastic public transportation, it very easy to get anywhere in the city via tram. Check out what all you can do in this amazing city below!

      Clubs & Bars

      1. De school

        Located in Amsterdam West De School is a former school turned into one of the coolest venues in Europe. It is a club, concert venue, restaurant, café, exhibition space, and gym. It is open 24/7 so always time to check it out during your visit.

      2. Air

        The club is a household name in Amsterdam’s nightlife and for good reason. It is ranked as one of the best 100 nightclubs in the world almost every year. Great place to go out and sure to have a good time.

      3. Shelter

        Shelter can be found in the most Southern tip of Amsterdam right behind Central Station. Once you go down the steps of the Shelter you’ll get to the club with great design and music in mind.

      4. Disco Dolly

        Disco Dolly is a mix between a nightclub and a bar. Also, open 7 days a week you can hear mostly disco, funk, and house music, but they will mix in some other sounds now and then. A fun place for a chill night out.

      5. Canvas @ Volkshotel

        A sweet rooftop lounge with food and drinks that shows some great views of Amsterdam. Every Friday and Saturday night around 10pm it turns into a great club as well.

      6. De Marktkantine

        Recently renovated this historical Dutch venue houses multiple dancing areas but continues to keep its classic flair. Great music here and can find good art and food as well.

      Museums

      Museumplein off the 2 tram. Even if you do not want to go to a museum there is a lovely square here with a great place to relax and ice skate in the winter.

      1. Moco

        This street art museum can sometimes be mistaken for “The Banksy Museum” for its extensive collection of Banksy pieces, but there are always wonderful works from amazing artists from across the world. With its ever changing exhibitions, you can also get to see the works of Icy and Sot and Basquiat.

      2. Van Gogh

        The museum houses a huge collection of Van Gogh pieces taking you through his life and times. In the springtime, one of the coolest parts of the museums is that it has a sunflower field where visitors can come and take as many flowers as they would like. Online tickets only!

      3. Stedelijk

        The museum for modern art, contemporary art, and design. Here you can see works from Van Gogh, Henri Matisse, Jackson Pollock, Andy Warhol, Willem de Kooning, and Lucio Fontana to name a few.

      4. Anne Frank House

        The Anne Frank Huis the old house of hers that has been turned into a museum dedicated to her and her work. The building is located on a canal called the Prinsengracht in central Amsterdam in the Netherlands. Be sure to book tickets at least two weeks early as this place is almost always sold out. Online tickets only!

      5. Rijksmuseum

        The Dutch national museum shows the art and history of the country and culture.

      Food

      1. Foodhallen

        A food hall where you can some all types of food. Oh, and the vibe is awesome.

      2. Original Stroopwafels

        The best food to come out of The Netherlands! Stroopwafels are wafer cookies with a caramel center. If this is already making your mouth water you can find them all over the city, but the best can be found at the Original Stroopwafels Located at Albert Cuyp Market

      3. Poffertjes

        Another Dutch Classic, these are essentially pancake bites. Like with stroopwafels you can find them in a lot of places, but the best ones are located at Albert Cuyp Market

      4. De Jappaner

        Some great Japanese food in Amsterdam. Great Japanese style finger foods and a great place for a late-night bite. Closes 1am on weekdays at 3am on the weekends.

      5. The Avocado Show

        If you’re into avocados this is truly the place for you. Here everything on the menu has avocados in, on, or around it. It is something that needs to be seen not read about.

      6. Kantjil De Tijger (Indonesian)

        Great Asian/Indonesian foods at a good price. A good place to come for lunch, dinner or a bite to go.

      7. Brasserie Ambassade

        Great food located in the center of Amsterdam. The vibe is also really nice with cool art everywhere. If you’re in the mood for a drink you can check out the restaurant's Library Bar.

      8. Bollywood (Indian)

        In a place where it can be hard to find good Indian food. This is definitely the place to go if you are in the mood.

      9. Grenada (Tapas)

        Traditional tapas with an all-you-can-eat option, plus Spanish grill dishes. Good for a small meal or food coma, you choose.

      Touristy but Worth it

      1. Canal Cruises

        A good relaxing way to see the entire city and get an understanding of the rich history of Amsterdam. Most tours last about an hour, take off right across from the central station and are very worth the time.

      2. Heineken Experience

        Take a tour of the Heineken facilities, where you can see the history of the brewing company and get to do some fun activities as well. A little gimmicky but still fun.

      3. A’dam Lookout

        At this lookout, you can an amazing view of the city from and if you’re daring enough, ride a swing that goes over the edge of the building.

      4. Visit Albert Cuyp Market

        The famed a street market on the Albert Cuypstraat the market is now open six days a week. The product selection at the market varies from the traditional range of clothing and even cameras. The famous Dutch Stroopwafels are prepared fresh here.

      5. Go to the Red Light District

        If you know you know…

      6. Visit Keukenhof Gardens

        Also known as the Garden of Europe, this is one of the world's largest flower gardens. You can see the famous Dutch tulips here and - no exaggeration - millions of other plants as well.

      7. Explore Jordaan

        A beautiful area in Amsterdam with a lot of art and history. A good place to walk see indie boutiques, cozy pubs, and hip eateries. There is also a small Cheese Museum in this area.

      Day Trips

      All of these locations are all within a train ride of Amsterdam

      1. Rotterdam

        Distance from Amsterdam: 40 minute train
        Destroyed by the effects of World War 2, Rotterdam has been rebuilt into the most modern city Holland featuring beautiful architecture everywhere you look. Old Dutch-style homes can still be found in Delfshaven, but in Rotterdam, there is much more than that. The main attractions here at the Cubed houses and Markethal both located in the city center. For a fun night out you can check out the street of Witte de Withstraat.

      2. Den Haag

        Distance from Amsterdam: 1 hour train
        The political capital of Holland, and the EU for that matter, there is a lot of culture and sites to see in Den Haag. There is also a nice beach and boardwalk where you can spend the day relaxing by the water.

      3. Utrecht

        Distance from Amsterdam: 30 minute train
        This historic city was once the most important city in The Netherlands and home to the largest university in The Netherlands – The University of Utrecht. With its own beautiful canal system, there is a lot to see here.

      4. Gouda

        Distance from Amsterdam: 50 minute train
        Home of the original Gouda Cheese, treacle waffles and the first Fairtrade street in the Netherlands. Gouda is the place to eat some classic Dutch meals and try some of the freshest Gouda you will ever try.

      5. Haarlem

        Distance from Amsterdam: 50 minute train
        The original Haarlem is a beautiful city known for its outlying tulip fields, art museums and hofjes (almshouses built around leafy courtyards).

Florence

At A Glance

Florence is a small city that packs a punch. At the heart of the city is the Duomo of Florence with most of the other tourist attractions relatively close by. With a lot to see in a relatively small area, you will probably run into most, if not all, the historic sites just walking around the downtown. Be sure to cross the Ponte Vecchio bridge to and sit at the Piazza le Michelangelo for a beautiful view of the city. Check out what else you can do in this amazing city below!

      Clubs

      1. Yab

        Most Fun: Monday
        This ultra-chic club brings in big crowds on Mondays and the weekends. This is always a student favorite but there is a the tough door policy.

      2. Discoteca Central Park (outdoor)

        This outdoor club is a great place if it’s warm. There are dance floors everywhere and even a Ferris wheel.

      3. Club TwentyOne

        Most Fun: Wednesday, Friday
        There is always going to be a good mix of locals, English speakers, and students at this club. It’s a fun club that can get rowdy with its drum and bass to Top 40 hits playing all night long.

      4. Space Club

        Most Fun: Saturday
        A great mix of locals and foreigners, but do not lose your cover ticket as you will not be allowed to leave until the club closes if you do, which you may end up doing even if you have the ticket.

      5. Flo

        Great view, great drinks, and great vibe. At this indoor/outdoor lounge club you should dress to impress which might also help your chances of getting in.

      6. The Blob Club

        This is a great club venue with a good mix of locals and tourists dancing all night long

      Bars

      1. Red Garter

        Most Fun: Tuesday
        The most popular American-style sports bar in Florence, there is enough space for any mood. There are beer pong tournaments here every Tuesday but be sure to sign up beforehand to get a spot. This is a fun bar to watch the big game, pregame, or even stay the whole night.

      2. Naima

        With the affordable drinks and consistent party atmosphere, there is always a fun crowd of local and international students. This place has a great vibe and a popular pregame spot.

      3. Bar Tabacchi Robin Hood

        Most fun: Monday
        A small tavern that can be a fun place for a chill night.

      4. Kikuya

        Kikuya is the oldest English pub in Florence with English football banners as wall decorations and first-rate beer selection - the Dragoon is some of the strongest beer you will ever have. This is a popular bar that will always have a young crowd and happy hour specials till 10pm.

      5. The Lion’s Fountain Irish Pub

        This is a very American bar, with college students being frequent guests and college apparel decorating the ceiling. This is a rowdy bar that is sure to bring memorable times any day of the week. Don’t forget to add a doodle on the wall.
        Monday Drink special: “buy a beer, get a shot”

      6. Astor Café

        Easily recognizable by the big orange clock in the window. You can find good frozen drinks and better Bellini’s.

      7. Eby’s

        Known for its great drinks and unique shots and for good reason. Eby is actually the guy who's personally preparing every single shot. The place is small so there can be a line sometimes. The affordable drinks and fun vibe make a great place to pregame.

      Food

      1. Antico Vinaio

        Highly rated as some of the best paninis in the world, this is a must visit in Florence. You will immediately recognize it by the line but don’t worry it moves fast. You can’t go wrong with any order there!

        ** Salumeria Verdi [locals call it Pino’s] is a good backup if the line is too long

      2. Gusta Pizza

        The best pizza in Florence. Don’t forget to ask them to make your pie in a heart shape.

      3. La Giostra

        This small but fancy restaurant is some of the best food you will have in Florence. The candlelit restaurant with walls covered in photographs of the restaurant owner and the famous guests that have visited you will quickly realize this is the place to be. Just don’t fill up on free appetizers and prosecco before your order comes. Also, make a reservation before going.

      4. Buca dell'Orafo

        This tiny hole in the wall is a great place to get authentic Italian food.

      5. Osteria Vini e Vecchi Sapori

        This is a small restaurant that is a great local experience. You’ll have to show up and see the menu of the day as it is always changing depending on what the chief is in the mood to make.

      6. Antica Sosta

        Good place for a quick cheap bite. Its 3 euro for panini and 5 euro for a salad and you choose what ingredients you want in it.

      7. Mercato Centrale

        This is a huge market with lots of sections and all types of food. Explore the whole market as you can find everything from pasta to fresh coffee with biscotti and everything in between. Some favorites here are Da Nerbone Sandwiches and Astor Café coffee.

      8. Mesopotamia Kebabs

        A good late night spot to get a nice quick one-two, or 3:00am bite

      9. Trattoria ZaZa

        Here you can find great food at a good price. This place is known for their pasta with truffle sauce, but you really can’t go wrong with any choice.

      Touristy but Worth it

      1. The Duomo

        The famous Duomo cathedral of Florence is a must visit. It’s possible to go inside and on top of the famed building if you are willing to wait on sometimes incredibly long lines. The top of the Duomo offers some great views of the whole city.

      2. Ponte Vecchio

        Translated to “Old Bridge” this is a picturesque spot looking at it or on it. You can find many shops along your crossing of the bridge.

      3. Boboli Gardens / Pitti Palace

        The other side of Ponte Vecchio is a beautiful garden area and place to hang out on a nice day. This is a popular spot so be ready to wait in line.

      4. Piazza le Michelangelo

        Here you can find one of the best views of the city with an unforgettable vibe. It’s a long walk up so if that’s not your thing you can take a cab up. Bring a nice bottle of wine, relax, and enjoy a beautiful sunset in Florence.

      5. Mercato Centrale

        This is a huge market with lots of sections and all types of food. Explore the whole market as you can find everything from pasta to fresh coffee with biscotti and everything in between. Even if you’re not looking to eat this is a cool place to check out.

Museums

  1. Gucci Garden
  2. Galleria dell’Academia
  3. Uffizi Gallery
    • ×

      Museums

      1. Gucci Garden

        This is the Gucci museum where you can go and see the history of Gucci fashion throughout its existence. There are beautiful pieces here and you can even get exclusive Gucci Garden Pieces when you’re here.

      2. Galleria dell’Academia

        This is where the famous Statue of David is located. It is a magnificent work, and there are countless other works here from the Italian golden age.

      3. Uffizi Gallery

        This is a huge art museum. There are so many famous pieces here from various time periods. It is closed on Monday and every other day is bound to have long lines, so see if you can get tickets online beforehand.

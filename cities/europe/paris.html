Paris

At A Glance

Paris is set up in the shape of a snail-shell with each artillery swirling around one another from 1 till 20. The 1st artillery is where the city center is having the Louvre, Arc de Triumph, and Royal Palace all located here. The 1st is also the most post posh area with many brands and stores located in this area, and then getting less and less fancy as the farther away you go. Besides the prominent tourist attractions, which all live up to the hype, there is a lot more to see and do in Paris. Check out what all you can do in this amazing city below!

      Clubs

      1. Rex Club

        Rex Club is a Parisian institution where you get a mix of powerful, funky sounds played by some of the hottest bands and DJs around. The dance floor is usually always packed, but if there are two side bars and some seated booths to give yourself a break before jumping back in.

      2. La Machine de Moulin Rouge

        La Machine du Moulin Rouge is a huge club that holds over 800 people. Post work the terrace bar draws a large crowd and it only gets bigger until the main dance floor opens up and the party begins. This can get pricey so if you want to save some money you can head next-door to La Machine, the sister club, where youâll get almost the same thrills at half the price.

      3. VIP Room

        VIP Room is one of the most exclusive clubs in Paris. The star-studded energy is apparent from the red carpet rolled out in honor of models, French Open tennis pros, and more. This makes the door policy super difficult, to put it lightly, but not impossible. Once inside the 3 story club has a huge dance floor area, numerous bars, and of course, a lot of space for VIPs.

      4. Le Baron Paris

        Le Baron is a small club, but packs a big punch. Because this is a small club it can be difficult to get in, but Le Baron stays open late, if you donât get in early you can try stopping later in the night.

      French Food

      1. Le Flandrin (16th)

        In an upscale neighborhood this restaurant has a good selection of food, drinks, and a friendly staff. If the weather is nice, you can eat outside on the terrace for a nice view as well.

      2. Girafe (16th)

        A fancy restaurant with good food and an even better view of the Eiffel Tower.

      3. Hotel Grand Amour (10th)

        Located in the Grand Amour this is a nice spot for breakfast, brunch, lunch, or dinner. Along with the eating area there is also a book-bar and aquarium located in the restaurant.

      4. Les Deux Magot (7th)

        Opened in 1885, this has been a staple for French food for over 100 years. Here youâll get a true Parisian food experience.

      5. Maison de la Truffe (9th)

        If you love truffles, you should not miss this restaurant. There are classic and experimental truffle dishes, and the desert is too good to pass up.

      6. Le Piaf (8th)

        This is a fun, delicious, and super chic dinner spot in Paris. The interior and staff are inviting so much so to where you might just start dancing during your dinner. The restaurant is open everyday except Monday from 7:00pm till 2:00am.

      7. Le Petit Keller (11th)

        This is a good spot for vegetarians/vegans as there are a lot of options, which can be hard to find in French food. All the food here is great with original flavors and plates to share.
